George Ou (who runs an informative blog if you have never visited it before) has an article about the nexgen hacking tools for cracking wireless networks… He also includes a list of best management practices to help keep your wireless network as secure as possible.

Visit here for the article.

One concern that I have is that hardware manufacturers are slow to update device firmware to support more secure technologies. Fortunately for me, my WAPs that I have deployed in my office are using WPA with AES encryption. Over the Christmas break, I will be replacing our firewall system with one built with IPCOP, including a more limited wireless network that will force users to VPN into the internal network.
